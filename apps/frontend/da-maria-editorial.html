Chi Siamo

Who
Are We

We believe in warmth that goes beyond the food — in creating spaces where people slow down, connect, and feel at home. — Maria, Chef & Founder
IMAGE
1200 × 675 px

Above. The kitchen team at service, circa estate 2025.

We're a team of food lovers, storytellers, and detail-obsessed dreamers who care deeply about every plate that leaves the kitchen. Behind every dish is a conversation, a memory, a moment worth sharing.

The restaurant was born from a very simple conviction: that Roman cuisine — when treated with honesty and without ceremony — becomes one of the great pleasures of the world. That conviction still governs everything we do.

✦

What you find on these tables is not performance. It is the accumulation of a thousand small decisions — about sourcing, timing, balance — made daily by people who remain, after all these years, genuinely interested in feeding you well.

the Kitchen

Scarlett trained in Napoli before spending a decade moving between the great trattorias of Lazio, absorbing a kind of cooking that is confident precisely because it has nothing to prove. Her menu changes when the market dictates, not before.

There is a rigour here that is invisible to the dining room — a standard maintained quietly, without announcement, in a kitchen where nobody raises their voice.

Il Giornale
Feature

On the Pleasure of Eating Slowly in a City That Never Quite Stops

There is a particular quality of afternoon light in Rome — honeyed, unhurried, falling across a marble table with the patience of something that has nothing to prove. This is the light we think about when we design a menu. Not for the eye, exactly, but for what it does to time.

Slow food, in Rome, is not a movement. It is a temperament. The slow meal is not an act of resistance; it is the default state. What visitors sometimes read as inefficiency is, in fact, a very old form of respect — respect for the food, for the table, and for the person sitting across from you.

From the Cellar

The Wines We're Pouring This Summer — and Why

The wine list at Scarlett is not a catalogue of prestige. It is a map of relationships — with producers who answer the phone in August, who send samples with handwritten notes, who remember what we asked for last harvest. This summer we are pouring lighter, as the heat demands.

A Vermentino from Sardegna that costs almost nothing and tastes like the sea. A Frascati that has been ignored for twenty years and is finally being reconsidered by people who grew up dismissing it. A Cerasuolo d'Abruzzo, cold, with the evening's secondi.

Essay

What the Market Taught Us About the Menu

We write the menu on Tuesday morning at Campo de' Fiori. Not from a list, not from a database of seasonal ingredients. From what is there, arranged by the vendor who has known Maria for eleven years, who puts the best things at the front and saves the very best for when he sees her coming.
